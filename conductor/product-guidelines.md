# Product Guidelines

## Communication & Prose Style
- **Professional & Direct:** Use concise, technical language that is accessible to system administrators. Avoid jargon where simpler terms suffice, but do not dumb down technical concepts.
- **Helpful & Guiding:** Provide inline explanations, tooltips, or "info" icons for complex operations (e.g., explaining RAID levels, Btrfs subvolume strategies, or Docker network modes).

## Visual Identity & Interaction
- **Material Design Principles:** Utilize a strict grid-based layout, clear typographic hierarchy, and meaningful transitions to create a structured and intuitive user experience.
- **High Information Density:** Design screens to efficiently display significant amounts of system data (tables, metrics, logs) without clutter. Use collapsible sections or tabs to manage complexity.
- **Immediate Feedback:** Employ toast notifications, progress bars, and optimistic UI updates to provide immediate confirmation of user actions and system state changes.
- **Dark-First Aesthetic:** Prioritize a dark theme suitable for long-term use in low-light environments, ensuring high contrast for readability of logs and metrics.
